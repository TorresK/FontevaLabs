Creating a Shipping Carrier and Delivery Method

Shipping Carriers refer to the vendors responsible for delivering customer orders. Examples of Shipping Carriers include:
  • US Postal Service
  • UPS
  • FedEx
A Delivery Method is the delivery option from that carrier. Examples of Delivery Methods include:
  • Overnight Air
  • Express 2-3 Day Ground
  • Standard 5-7 Day Ground
Navigate to the related Business Group record and ensure the desired shipping configurations are set:
  • Set up Shipping GL Account
  • Check the Enable Shipping by Order Total checkbox to allow customers to see a single shipping rate for the entire order. If left unchecked, customers will instead see shipping rates for each unique item in their order. In both cases, shipping is calculated only for the items that have Require Shipping enabled. For new Business Groups, the default is checked.
  • Use the Shipping Rate Basis picklist to calculate the shipping rate based on Item Weight or Item Value. For new Business Groups, the default value is Item Weight.

  1. Navigate to the Item Classes tab in the eStore app and click New.
  2. Enter the Shipping Carrier Name.
    Optionally, enter a Description.
  3. Select the Business Group.
  4. Check the Is Active and Is Shipping Carrier checkboxes.
  5. Select the Shipping GL Account and click Save.
    All subsequent Shipping Rate items created using the same Shipping Carrier will inherit the related Shipping GL Account. However, Admins can override the inheritance by selecting an alternative GL account on the Shipping Rate item itself.

Creating the Delivery Method

  1. Navigate to the Delivery Methods related list and click New Delivery Method.
    Each Shipping Carrier can have multiple Delivery Methods. For example, FedEx may have both “Overnight Air” and “Express 2-3 Day Ground" as delivery options.
  2. Enter the Delivery Method Name, Description, and click Save.
    The Shipping Carrier lookup field is autopopulated.
The Shipping Carrier and Delivery Method are created.
